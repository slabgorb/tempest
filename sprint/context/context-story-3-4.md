## Title
Difficulty ramp continues past the geometry cycle

## Problem
The 16-geometry roster (Task 1) cycles with period 16: at level 17, the game repeats the level-1 circle. Without explicit difficulty scaling past level 16, the geometry repetition would falsely signal an "end" to progression. The arcade's design keeps escalating: speeds rise, spawning accelerates, and the enemy roster (Tankers, Spikers, Pulsars, Fuseballs) opens up progressively — so returning players see new challenges even on repeated geometries.

**Spec references** (`docs/superpowers/specs/2026-06-24-tempest-clone-design.md`):
- L51–56: difficulty ramp (speed, spawn rates, enemy mix) driven by level number, no reset on geometry wrap.
- L143: "difficulty ramps level-to-level; the same geometry at level 1 vs level 17 plays much harder."

## Technical Approach
_High-level only — Dev owns implementation detail._
- The existing `levelParams(level)` ramp formula (`1 + (level-1)*0.15`) already scales monotonically, so no reset is needed — just ensure it is applied to all levels, including those beyond 16.
- `rollSpawnKind(level, rng)` introduces enemy types (Tanker, Spiker) at level 3 and two more (Pulsar, Fuseball) at level 5, but does not scale their *weight* past the first cycle. On the second cycle (levels 17–32), increase the weights of the harder kinds proportionally to `cycle = floor((level - 1) / 16)`, keeping the early-level introduction schedule intact.
- Tests verify: monotonic speed/spawn ramp, floors on timing intervals so very high levels stay playable, and full roster availability by level 18.

## Scope
- **In scope:** extending `levelParams` and `rollSpawnKind` to make difficulty explicit past level 16; unit tests covering the monotonic ramp and late-cycle roster escalation.
- **Out of scope:** geometry table (Task 1), warp mechanics (Tasks 2–3), palette/render (Task 5), or gameplay tuning of the constants themselves.

## Acceptance Criteria
_Starting set — Dev refines during implementation._
1. `levelParams` returns strictly increasing speed and spawn-rate metrics for consecutive levels across the cycle boundary (e.g., level 20 > level 16).
2. Timing intervals (`flipInterval`, `spawnInterval`, `pulseInterval`) have playable floors: even at level 50, intervals stay above 0.3s/1.2s respectively (no infinite spawn).
3. By level 18 (into the second cycle), `rollSpawnKind` can generate all five enemy kinds (Flipper, Tanker, Spiker, Pulsar, Fuseball) with seeded RNG over enough rolls.
4. The early-level introduction schedule is preserved: Tanker/Spiker unavailable before level 3, Pulsar/Fuseball unavailable before level 5.
5. Deterministic: identical seed and RNG state produce identical spawn sequences across all levels.
6. All existing tests remain green; new tests in `tests/core/sim.difficulty.test.ts` pass.
